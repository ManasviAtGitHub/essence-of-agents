# F7 - The dated atlas

*Verified as of 2026-07. Exhibits age; the map does not.*

Six modules taught the mechanisms from first principles. This one places every
2026 headline from the track - plus the tail the modules skipped - on a single
dated map, each pinned to the principle it attacks. It is the attack-map made
filterable: the page that ages, pointing at the mapping that does not.

## Question
You have met about fifty headlines across F1-F6 - sparse and linear attention,
RL everywhere, trained loops, robot bodies, diffusion and world models. How do
they relate? And when a new one drops next week, where does it go?

## Principle
**Every frontier headline attacks a principle you already own.** So the whole
zoo sorts on two axes:
- **the principle it attacks** - one of a handful of beliefs the earlier tracks
  installed: *the model only reads text* (F1), *attention must compare against &
  remember everything* (F2), *training is a fixed row of stages* (F3), *you
  hand-write the agent's loop* (F4), *an agent acts through software & senses
  text* (F5), *predict the next token, left to right* (F6), and *the surrounding
  stack is ad-hoc plumbing* (the tail).
- **the lane** it sits in - which chapter of the track owns its mechanism.

Cross the two and the frontier stops being fifty miracles. Filter by one
principle and you watch a single belief get hit from several lanes at once - and
sometimes **defended**: the best image generator is autoregressive (tokens
beating diffusion), MiniMax M2 reverted to full attention, Figure Helix keeps
motion continuous. Those counter-exhibits are marked, not hidden (rule 12).

## Dated exhibits (rule 10)
The atlas holds ~46 pins spanning Feb 2025 -> mid 2026, one per headline in
F1-F6, plus the tail: MCP -> the Agentic AI Foundation, A2A v1.0, Microsoft
Agent 365, Claude Cowork / ChatGPT Agent, Nemotron 3, Antigravity, and a
handful of thinly sourced items (Gemini Diffusion, K2.6 300-agent swarms, OpenAI
universal verifiers, Llama 4 / Behemoth shelved) shown but flagged UNVERIFIED.
Every pin carries the month it landed and its source; the header states the
"verified as of" date.

## See it (no key)
`widgets/dated-atlas/index.html` - a filterable, dated atlas (the same grammar as
the models-track atlas, with time added):
- a **timeline rail** plots the currently-shown pins across 2025 -> 2026, colored
  by lane, hollow-dashed where UNVERIFIED, gold-ringed for counters;
- two filter rows - **attacks** (the principle) and **lane** (the chapter);
- a card grid, newest first, each pin dated and tagged; click any pin (card or
  timeline dot) for its one-screen detail (what it is, what it attacks or
  defends, lane, month, source).

## The aha
The frontier is not fifty separate shocks - it is a handful of principles, each
attacked (and occasionally defended) from several directions, unfolding over
eighteen months. Name the principle a headline attacks and it already has a home.

## Honest notes
- Dates, sizes, $ figures and tok/s are the labs' own claims - narrative color,
  never measured here (rule 11).
- Thinly sourced items are shown but flagged UNVERIFIED (rule 10); the map has
  retreats and dead ends too (M2's revert, Behemoth shelved).
- Counter-exhibits are marked, not dropped - the atlas teaches the argument, not
  a winner (rule 12). The modules (F1-F6) own the mechanisms; this page only
  places them.

## Done when (the bar for this module)
Given an unseen 2026 headline, you can name the principle it attacks and the lane
it belongs in - and say whether it is an attack or a counter. `CHALLENGE.md`.
